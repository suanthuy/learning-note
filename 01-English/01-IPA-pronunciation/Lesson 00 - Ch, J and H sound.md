# /ʧ/ ("ch") - voiceless and /dʒ/ ("j") - voice

## /tʃ/ ("ch") - voiceless

- **Mouth Position**: Pucker your lips slightly and push them forward, similar to the /ʃ/ (sh) sound.
- **Tongue Position**: Place the tip of your tongue on the alveolar ridge (roof of the mouth behind the upper teeth) to stop the air, similar to /t/.
- **Release**: Release the air slowly while moving the tongue slightly back, creating a noisy friction (like the "ch" in "church").
- **Voiceless**: Do not use your voice (throat); only push air out. 

## /dʒ/ ("j") - voice

- **Position**: Place the tip of your tongue on the roof of your mouth, just behind your upper front teeth (alveolar ridge).
- **Shape**: Pucker your lips slightly and keep them in a rounded position.
- **Action**: Build up air pressure and release it slowly while voicing (vibrating your vocal cords).
- **Result**: It sounds like the "j" in jam or the "g" in germ.

## H sound

When H sound comes in the beginning of word, it will pronoun with a strong loud airstream. First breath in and then loudly out.

	Example: 
		Hand, Hide, Hope, Hair, House, Harvard, Honey, Happy, Who, Whole
		H word pairs: old - hold, is - his, it - hit, at - hat, arm - harm, ill - hill, ate - hate, as - has
